අප ගැන - About Us

This web application provides an easy way to search and navigate the Budda Jayanthi Tipitaka (BJT) scanned pages. It uses a search index created from the Table of Contents (ToC - පටුන) of the 57 Tipitaka volumes. Hal akuru were used where appropriate to ease the Sinhala reading. Other than that no changes were made to the original ToC entries.

BJT is the most trusted Tipitaka source by scolars worldwide and the Sinhala translation is the most trusted in Sri Lanka. The scanned PDFs of the all 57 volumes of BJT are available here (900 MB download).

It is entirely possible that there are some mistakes in the search index and thus when clicking on a sutta name it opens up the wrong page. If you find one feel free to send us an email at adminpitaka.lk. We are also looking for volunteers who can build Android and iOS apps for this web application.

Usage Instructions

  • You can use Sinhala Unicode, or Singlish for searching the Table of Contents. (Singlish letter table is listed below)
  • At least 2 characters need to be typed in the search text box to activate the searching process. If the searching is not activated press 'Enter' or an arrow key.
  • Results from the Table of Contents of BJT volumes matching your search term will be listed in a table view. Maximum 100 results will be shown.
  • You can change the order of displayed entries by clicking on the and icons in the table heading.
  • You can limit the searching to a subset of the available 57 books by clicking on the icon.
  • By clicking on the icon you can view the results of the last search.
  • You can also navigate to a paricular section/sutta in the Tipitaka by using the Tree View. To show/hide the tree click on the icon.
  • Click the button with icon in the toolbar to open/close the top banner.
